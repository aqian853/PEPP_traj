Abstract Format:

Submit here: Google forms link

Abstracts should be submitted in the following WORD File format:

  • Microsoft Word Document (docx)
  • 1.5 spacing, 1” margins
  • Word limit is 250, Font size: 11 (Arial)
  • Title, subheadings, and names DO NOT count towards the word count.
  • The abstract should have the following sections, and these section headings must be in bold.
  • Background or Introduction
  • Hypothesis or Purpose
  • Methods
  • Results
  • Conclusion(s)
  • Title must be in the centered alignment format. Do not add it as a Header
  • Your name and supervisor’s name must be under the title of the abstract. Do not add it as a Header.
  • The abstract section must be in the justified alignment format
  • Do not include jpegs, colour graphs, figures, or references in your abstract. Only text will be included in the SURP abstract booklet.
  • File Naming Convention:
  • Podium Abstract: First Name LastName_SURP_PodiumAbstract.docx
  • Poster Abstract: FirstName LastName _SURP_PosterAbstract.docx

Suggested CDSS Cutoffs

5, 6, 7, 8 came up in my search (note: some of the studies are focused on cross-cultural assessment of the scale, which may impact how relevant their findings are for our purposes)

Clinical utility of the Calgary Depression Scale for Schizophrenia in individuals at ultra-high risk of psychosis: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S092099641730395X

Authors suggest that a cutoff of 7 has a “Youden's index with a sensitivity of 94.7% and a specificity of 72.1% for detecting MDD in individuals with ultra-high risk of psychosis. The likelihood ratio (LR) was 3.40 for a positive test result and 0.07 for a negative test result” with this cut off.

Cross-cultural adaptation and validation of the Spanish version of the Calgary Depression Scale for Schizophrenia: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0920996402004905

“The most suitable cutoff score to discriminate between depressed and nondepressed patients was 5, with a sensitivity of 94.7%, specificity of 86.5%, positive predictive value of 70%, negative predictive value of 98%, and likelihood ratios for a positive and a negative test of 7.01 and 0.07, respectively.”

The Persian version of the Calgary Depression Scale for Schizophrenia (CDSS-P): https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1876201819306082

                Suggests cutoff of 8

Prevalence of comorbid depression in schizophrenia: A meta-analysis of observational studies: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0165032719327624

Most studies included in the review used a cutoff of >6 or >=7 (24 of their studies out of 46)
